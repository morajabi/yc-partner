# YC Partner

YC Partner is a public knowledge pack and agent skill for reviewing YC applications and preparing for YC interviews. It helps founders apply to Y Combinator with a fresh, source-grounded second pair of eyes.

It is based on official YC resources, Paul Graham's essays, interviews with YC partners, insights from Jessica Livingston, founder stories, example applications, and related public material.

## Get started

Install or load the plugin/skill in your agent environment, then ask:

```text
Use YC Partner to review my YC application
```

Then provide your application text or a path to a Markdown file.

Optionally, you can provide paths to your demo video and founder video. For video review, the skill only needs the video paths and an `OPENAI_TOKEN` available in your local environment; it handles transcription internally. Do not paste secrets into chat or commit `.env` files, generated transcripts, extracted audio, or private application material.

## How (not) to use it

Use this as a second pair of eyes: a fresh reviewer that gives you a high-level perspective on what you wrote. Founders are often too close to their product and know too much; they can become blind to descriptions that are too complicated for a YC partner to understand quickly. This skill aims to help you close that gap.

Please do not ask this to rewrite your YC application. Do not copy-paste text generated by your agent. Plain copy-paste is likely to decrease the quality of your application because the LLM has no deep insight into your company or your unique angle. It may gravitate toward trendy or impressive-sounding ideas when suggesting one-liners, and could mislead you into thinking those are better than your human-written descriptions.

## Principles

- Official YC sources are the highest authority for YC-specific guidance.
- Essays, blog posts, and interviews with YC partners, Paul Graham, Jessica Livingston, and similar YC-affiliated sources are also included in the review process.
- Anecdotes from successful YC founders like Patrick and John Collison, Amjad Masad, Brian Chesky, and others may be used as founder stories when relevant.
- The skill is capable of browsing YC Requests for Startups and funded companies to provide additional context if your idea falls into those areas. It understands these are not the only ideas YC is interested in.
- Application reviews are direct, specific, and source-grounded based on the resources and compiled guidance derived from them.
- The skill should cite local resource files and public URLs when making guidance-based claims.

## Repository layout

- `skills/yc-partner/` contains the installed skill and embedded processed Markdown corpus.
- `skills/yc-partner/resources/` contains durable source files only: transcripts, essays, posts, examples, and notes converted to clean Markdown with metadata.
- `skills/yc-partner/indexes/` maps application questions, interview topics, FAQs, and source types to relevant resources.
- `skills/yc-partner/guides/generated/` is for autogenerated summaries from indexes and resources.
- `skills/yc-partner/guides/curated/` is for human-written guidance that scripts must not overwrite.
- `scripts/` contains maintainer tools for collecting, transcribing, extracting, indexing, and generating corpus files.
- `templates/` contains maintainer templates for new processed source notes.
