# YC Partner

YC Partner is a public knowledge pack and agent skill for answering YC application questions, reviewing YC applications, and preparing for YC interviews. It helps founders apply to Y Combinator with source-grounded answers, a fresh second pair of eyes, and a few focused startup lessons tied to their actual company.

It is based on official YC resources, Paul Graham's essays, interviews with YC partners, insights from Jessica Livingston, founder stories, example applications, and related public material.

## Get started

Install or load the plugin/skill in your agent environment, then ask:

```text
Use YC Partner to review my YC application
```

Then provide your application text or a path to a Markdown file.

You can also use it before you have a finished draft:

```text
Use YC Partner to answer my questions before I write my YC application
```

Optionally, you can provide paths to your demo video and founder video. For video review, the skill only needs the video paths and an `OPENAI_TOKEN` available in your local environment; it handles transcription internally. Do not paste secrets into chat or commit `.env` files, generated transcripts, extracted audio, or private application material.

## Before You Apply

YC Partner can answer common pre-application questions and doubts with sources, before you have a finished application. For example: whether it is too early to apply with just an idea, whether YC funds solo founders or nontechnical founders, whether a non-Ivy or international background matters, whether a sector is YC-relevant, whether to apply late or to a future batch, and how to think about rejection or reapplying.

Answers should be direct and source-grounded. For YC-specific claims, the skill should prefer official YC resources first, then add relevant partner essays, tweets, posts, interviews, or founder stories when they help. It should end with a focused set of 1-4 resources to read, usually 1-2, not a broad reading list.

## How (not) to use it

Use this as a second pair of eyes: a fresh reviewer that gives you a high-level perspective on what you wrote. Founders are often too close to their product and know too much; they can become blind to descriptions that are too complicated for a YC partner to understand quickly. This skill aims to help you close that gap.

Please do not ask this to rewrite your YC application. Do not copy-paste text generated by your agent. Plain copy-paste is likely to decrease the quality of your application because the LLM has no deep insight into your company or your unique angle. It may gravitate toward trendy or impressive-sounding ideas when suggesting one-liners, and could mislead you into thinking those are better than your human-written descriptions.

## Office Hours

For broad first-pass reviews, YC Partner should also include an `Office Hours` section. This is a small educational layer drawn from the bundled source corpus: 1-4 tightly related nuggets, usually 1-2, that help the founder deepen their understanding of startups in the context of their actual company and profile.

These are not broad reading lists. They should be short, specific notes with a relevant quote, story, or insight and a link to the full resource. Good nuggets might connect the application to user insight, growth, revenue quality, idea selection, founder-market fit, pitch clarity, confidence, commitment, work history, sector context, or tar pit risk.

## Fun Facts

For broad first-pass reviews, YC Partner should include one short `Fun Fact` item from `skills/yc-partner/guides/fun-facts.md`. These are source-linked YC stats, historical notes, and insider-context nuggets. They should be lightweight and clearly separate from the critique.

## Principles

- Official YC sources are the highest authority for YC-specific guidance.
- Essays, blog posts, and interviews with YC partners, Paul Graham, Jessica Livingston, and similar YC-affiliated sources are also included in the review process.
- Anecdotes from successful YC founders like Patrick and John Collison, Amjad Masad, Brian Chesky, and others may be used as founder stories when relevant.
- The skill is capable of browsing YC Requests for Startups and funded companies to provide additional context if your idea falls into those areas. It understands these are not the only ideas YC is interested in.
- The skill can help founders before they finish writing by answering common YC doubts and pointing to the smallest relevant source set.
- Application reviews are direct, specific, and source-grounded based on the resources and compiled guidance derived from them.
- Broad first-pass reviews should include focused `Office Hours` learning notes that are directly relevant to the founder, company, and stage.
- Broad first-pass reviews should include one short source-linked `Fun Fact` item.
- The skill should cite local resource files and public URLs when making guidance-based claims.

## Repository layout

- `skills/yc-partner/` contains the installed skill and embedded processed Markdown corpus.
- `skills/yc-partner/resources/` contains durable source files only: transcripts, essays, posts, examples, and notes converted to clean Markdown with compact metadata and full captured content.
- `skills/yc-partner/resources/application-form/` contains official YC application form questions and batch-specific prompt captures.
- `skills/yc-partner/resources/rfs/` contains official YC Requests for Startups sections split into one processed Markdown file per RFS.
- `skills/yc-partner/resources/tweets/` contains public tweets and X threads converted to clean Markdown with compact metadata.
- `skills/yc-partner/indexes/` maps application questions, interview topics, FAQs, and source types to relevant resources.
- `skills/yc-partner/guides/` contains maintained Markdown guides updated as resources change. Automated updates and optional human-curated notes should be merged into these files, not split into required paired folders.
- `scripts/` contains maintainer tools for collecting, transcribing, extracting, indexing, and generating corpus files.
- `templates/` contains maintainer templates for new processed source notes.
