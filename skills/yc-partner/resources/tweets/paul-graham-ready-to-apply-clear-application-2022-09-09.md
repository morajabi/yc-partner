## Summary

- Paul Graham says founders are ready to apply to YC if they have an idea; traction is not required. He says YC prefers good founders without traction to bad founders with traction. He also says the most important quality in an application is clarity: after reading vague marketing language, partners are delighted by applications that get to the point.

### Tweet 1

Paul Graham (@paulg), Sep 9, 2022:

Are you ready yet to apply to YC? If you have an idea, the answer is yes. That's all you need. Now that YC has become more established, a lot of people feel they need "traction" before they apply. But that's false.

Timestamp shown in capture: 10:05 PM, Sep 9, 2022.

### Reply 1

Paul Graham (@paulg), Sep 9, 2022:

YC prefers good founders without traction to bad founders with. So if you're a good founder, just apply.

### Reply 2

Paul Graham (@paulg), Sep 9, 2022:

You know what's the most important thing your application can be? Clear. After reading ten applications full of vague marketing-speak about disrupting x and platforms for y, the partners are delighted when they see one that simply gets to the point.
