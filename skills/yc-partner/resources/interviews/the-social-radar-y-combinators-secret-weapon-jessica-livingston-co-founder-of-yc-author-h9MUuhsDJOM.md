- Use for founder and interview evaluation: cofounder dynamics, commitment, defensiveness, earnestness, domain expertise, founder-market fit, and whether a technical founder is treated as an equal.
- Jessica explains the "social radar" role in early YC interviews: while technical partners evaluated product and technology, she watched social cues, whether founders got along, whether they were committed, and whether they got defensive.
- Red flags: defensive answers, a business founder who appears to have a "hacker in a cage," founders without authentic connection to the problem, and teams that cannot explain why their product is better without educating calmly.
- Green flags: authentic domain connection, the ability to take questions without defensiveness, earnestness toward users and the problem, contagious determination, and signs that the founders will "make things happen."
- Anecdote: Jessica says the Airbnb founders' cereal boxes showed unusual hustle and made her believe they would do whatever they could to make the company succeed, even though Paul Graham disliked the original idea.
- Anecdote: Jessica remembers another team where the exact startup did not work, but the same founders later pivoted into GOAT; the memorable signal was that the founders themselves were going to make something succeed.
- Practical reviewer note: use this source when an application or mock interview feels technically impressive but socially off, when one founder dominates, when defensiveness appears, or when the team needs to show authentic connection to the user.
