## Sample Rewrites

Use when: one answer is confusing, too generic, too long, evasive, jargon-heavy, or the user asks for a better version.

- Start with the founder's quoted original sentence or phrase when useful.
- Give one concise sample rewrite only when facts are grounded in supplied text, public evidence, or founder-provided context.
- Mark rewrites as directional examples, not final copy.
- If facts are missing, give a fill-in scaffold or ask the missing question.
- Do not rewrite many answers at once unless the user explicitly drills down into rewriting.

Good rewrite targets:

- One-line company description.
- "What are you making?"
- "How far along are you?"
- "Why this idea?"
- "Who are your competitors?"
- Founder bio/profile when the facts are present but buried.
- Founder/demo video script excerpt, not a full script.

Avoid:

- Full-application rewrites.
- Invented traction, customer pain, market size, insight, or founder history.
- Polished-sounding prose that loses founder specificity.
