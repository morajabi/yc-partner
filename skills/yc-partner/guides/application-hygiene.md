# Application Hygiene Guide

Use this as one explicit pass in every broad YC application review, after the substantive product, founder, market, and traction critique. Report only material issues; if the draft is clean, one line is enough: `Hygiene: no obvious required-field, typo, link, video, or demo issues found in the provided draft.`

## Checklist

- Complete the application. Required fields are marked in the official form capture, including founder video, company name, and the 50-character company description (`../resources/application-form/fall-2026-application-form-questions.md:19`, `../resources/application-form/fall-2026-application-form-questions.md:30`, `../resources/application-form/fall-2026-application-form-questions.md:35`, `../resources/application-form/fall-2026-application-form-questions.md:36`). Startup School says to fill the whole thing out, including founder biographies, because missing basics read as low effort (`../resources/yc-youtube/how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-startup-school-B5tU2447OK8.md:126`).
- Fix obvious mechanics. Grammar, punctuation, capitalization, and typos matter because they signal care and seriousness (`../resources/yc-youtube/how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-startup-school-B5tU2447OK8.md:129`). Dalton also calls out empty answers, missing URLs/bios, and errors as common avoidable weaknesses (`../resources/yc-youtube/how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-by-dalton-caldwell-8yiOcCPvyNE.md:151`).
- Answer the prompt directly. YC says written answers should be clear and concise; more words do not help if the reader cannot quickly tell who the founders are, what exists today, whether there are users, and what is special (`../resources/yc-youtube/how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-startup-school-B5tU2447OK8.md:137`, `../resources/yc-youtube/how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-startup-school-B5tU2447OK8.md:140`). PG’s highest-level application advice is the same: clarity matters most, and applications that get to the point stand out (`../resources/tweets/paul-graham-ready-to-apply-clear-application-2022-09-09.md:48`).
- Keep the tone matter-of-fact. Prefer concrete nouns, current facts, and numbers over sales language. Dalton says great applications are easy to read because there is no jargon and everything adds up; weak ones make readers work to infer the basics (`../resources/yc-youtube/how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-by-dalton-caldwell-8yiOcCPvyNE.md:190`).
- Make the first product explanation immediately understandable. PG’s presentation advice is useful here as communication mechanics: say what you are doing as soon as possible, and prefer a narrow concrete description to a vague broad one (`../resources/pg-essays/how-to-present-to-investors.md:59`, `../resources/pg-essays/how-to-present-to-investors.md:93`). Garry Tan’s interview framing is a good two-sentence test: what problem, for whom, how the solution works, why this team, and how big it can get (`../resources/yc-partners/garry-tan-3-tips-to-nail-the-y-combinator-interview-rfTgzA6iKZc.md:33`, `../resources/yc-partners/garry-tan-3-tips-to-nail-the-y-combinator-interview-rfTgzA6iKZc.md:38`).
- Attach the founder video and follow the founder-video directions. YC says it should introduce the founders, what they are doing and why, and contain only founders talking (`../resources/yc-website/application-video.md:19`, `../resources/yc-website/application-video.md:20`). All founders should appear, use bullet points instead of reading a script, and do not submit a product demo as the founder video (`../resources/yc-website/application-video.md:21`, `../resources/yc-website/application-video.md:22`, `../resources/yc-website/application-video.md:23`).
- Treat time limits as reader-respect constraints, not editing trivia. The founder video page says one minute (`../resources/yc-website/application-video.md:20`), while the application form separately asks demo uploads to stay within 3 minutes / 100 MB (`../resources/application-form/fall-2026-application-form-questions.md:38`). If a video or demo runs long, it should be because each extra second adds concrete founder, product, user, or traction signal.
- Attach a demo or product link when available, and include credentials if needed. The form asks for a demo if one exists, a product link if any, and login credentials when required (`../resources/application-form/fall-2026-application-form-questions.md:38`, `../resources/application-form/fall-2026-application-form-questions.md:40`, `../resources/application-form/fall-2026-application-form-questions.md:41`).
- Remove obfuscation and unsupported claims. YC’s application readers need to retell the company story from the application; if they cannot tell what is built, who built it, whether users exist, or what the current state is, the application is weak (`../resources/yc-youtube/how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-startup-school-B5tU2447OK8.md:163`). Misleading revenue, traction, background, or education claims are disqualifying, and extraordinary claims need evidence (`../resources/yc-youtube/how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-startup-school-B5tU2447OK8.md:187`, `../resources/yc-youtube/how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-startup-school-B5tU2447OK8.md:195`).
