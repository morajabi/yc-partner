# Interview Prep Guide

Use this guide after reviewing the application.

## Drill Areas

- What do you do?
- Who needs it badly?
- What proof do you have?
- Why are users switching or trying it?
- Why now?
- Why will this become big?
- What sequence of markets gets you from the first users to a big company?
- Why will incumbents not win by default?
- What did you learn from users?
- Why this team?
- What is the biggest risk?

## Core Mechanics

YC interviews are short. The official Startup School video describes them as a 10-minute Zoom with all founders present, while YC has the application open and asks basic, context-dependent questions (`../resources/yc-youtube/how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-startup-school-B5tU2447OK8.md:226`).

Prepare around:

- Team, market, and traction (`../resources/tweets/michael-seibel-yc-interview-prep-top-ten-2015-10-31.md:23`).
- Problem, solution/team fit, and how big the company can become (`../resources/yc-partners/garry-tan-3-tips-to-nail-the-y-combinator-interview-rfTgzA6iKZc.md:36`).
- Truth-based conviction: founders should know why the startup is worth believing, know their market, and avoid vague pitch language (`../resources/pg-essays/how-to-convince-investors.md:82`, `../resources/pg-essays/how-to-convince-investors.md:89`, `../resources/pg-essays/how-to-convince-investors.md:225`).
- Presentation clarity, with caveat: investor presentation advice is useful for communication drills but does not override official YC interview guidance. Use it to practice immediate descriptions, narrow wording, specific numbers, and user stories (`../resources/pg-essays/how-to-present-to-investors.md:59`, `../resources/pg-essays/how-to-present-to-investors.md:93`, `../resources/pg-essays/how-to-present-to-investors.md:236`, `../resources/pg-essays/how-to-present-to-investors.md:247`).
- A clear 30-second "what do you do?" answer with an example and no jargon (`../resources/tweets/michael-seibel-yc-interview-prep-top-ten-2015-10-31.md:32`).
- Numbers, customer stories, competitors, alternatives, revenue, gross margin or take rate, growth, burn, pricing, NPS, and retention where applicable (`../resources/yc-partners/garry-tan-3-tips-to-nail-the-y-combinator-interview-rfTgzA6iKZc.md:51`).
- Recent product progress since applying. Gustaf Alstromer says one favorite YC question is what founders have done for the product since applying (`../resources/interviews/lessons-from-working-with-600-yc-startups-gustaf-alstrmer-y-combinator-airbnb-ZoKLofsp8u0.md:921`, `../resources/interviews/lessons-from-working-with-600-yc-startups-gustaf-alstrmer-y-combinator-airbnb-ZoKLofsp8u0.md:922`).
- Non-defensive pressure. Jessica Livingston treats defensive answers as a red flag and green-lights authentic domain connection, calm answers, earnestness, and signs that founders will make things happen (`../resources/interviews/the-social-radar-y-combinators-secret-weapon-jessica-livingston-co-founder-of-yc-author-h9MUuhsDJOM.md:20`, `../resources/interviews/the-social-radar-y-combinators-secret-weapon-jessica-livingston-co-founder-of-yc-author-h9MUuhsDJOM.md:21`).
- External anecdote only: the CodeAnt AI story is useful as a reminder that product, metrics, growth, and differentiation must be explainable under interview pressure, but it is not YC policy about post-rejection replies (`../resources/external/codeant-ai-yc-rejection-to-acceptance-story.md:17`, `../resources/external/codeant-ai-yc-rejection-to-acceptance-story.md:37`, `../resources/external/codeant-ai-yc-rejection-to-acceptance-story.md:43`).
- External example only: the Kudu rejection email is useful for drilling incumbent-market answers. If the company is improving on a product everyone already uses, practice why users will notice, switch, and pay despite embedded workflows (`../resources/external/kudu-yc-s22-rejection-email-slack-alternative-2022-06-27.md:15`, `../resources/external/kudu-yc-s22-rejection-email-slack-alternative-2022-06-27.md:31`).

## Practice Style

Keep answers short. Interrupt vague answers. Ask for numbers, examples, and user behavior.

Prefer realistic pressure over scripted polish.

Most answers should be 20-30 seconds, with follow-up questions expected (`../resources/tweets/michael-seibel-yc-interview-prep-top-ten-2015-10-31.md:28`). Practice being interrupted and answer in inverted pyramid style: answer directly first, then add context if there is time (`../resources/yc-partners/garry-tan-3-tips-to-nail-the-y-combinator-interview-rfTgzA6iKZc.md:87`).

Do not train founders into memorized speeches. The official Startup School video warns that overprepared, robotic, misleading, or adversarial interview behavior can backfire (`../resources/yc-youtube/how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-startup-school-B5tU2447OK8.md:243`).

Before the mock interview, decide which founder will cover technical details and which founder will cover business details; founders should not talk over or contradict each other (`../resources/tweets/michael-seibel-yc-interview-prep-top-ten-2015-10-31.md:36`). Prepare crisp answers for red flags, but do not dwell on them (`../resources/tweets/michael-seibel-yc-interview-prep-top-ten-2015-10-31.md:38`).

Ask founder-dynamics questions deliberately: how the founders met, whether they have worked together, whether the technical founder has real agency, and whether the team can answer without one person suppressing another (`../resources/interviews/the-social-radar-y-combinators-secret-weapon-jessica-livingston-co-founder-of-yc-author-h9MUuhsDJOM.md:209`, `../resources/interviews/the-social-radar-y-combinators-secret-weapon-jessica-livingston-co-founder-of-yc-author-h9MUuhsDJOM.md:556`).
