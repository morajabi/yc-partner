# Question Bank

Use this guide to generate source-grounded questions for office-hours sessions, YC interview prep, investor meeting prep, and founder operating check-ins.

## How To Use

- Pick one mode: office hours, YC interview, investor meeting, operating review, hiring, or user/customer discovery.
- Ask fewer, sharper questions. Do not dump the whole bank.
- For mock interviews, interrupt vague answers and ask follow-ups.
- For office hours, prefer questions that expose the current bottleneck and next experiment.

## Office-Hours Questions

- What is holding the company back from moving faster?
- What did you try in the last two weeks, and what happened?
- What user/customer fact would change your mind?
- What are you avoiding because it is uncomfortable?
- What would you do this week if fundraising, press, and hiring were off the table?
- Which goal did you miss, and why?
- If this is an AI company, which part is still manual, and what must you learn before scaling it?
- Are you selling to the smallest customer segment that can give fast feedback, or the largest logos that slow the learning loop?

Use with the office-hours guide and Gustaf Alstromer's descriptions of YC office-hours accountability (`../guides/office-hours.md`, `../resources/interviews/lessons-from-working-with-600-yc-startups-gustaf-alstrmer-y-combinator-airbnb-ZoKLofsp8u0.md:247`).

## User Discovery Questions

- Who has the problem most painfully today?
- How are they solving it now?
- What did they do last time the problem happened?
- What do they pay in money, time, risk, or workflow pain?
- What would make them switch this week?
- What did users tell you that surprised you?
- What did users do that contradicted what they said?
- Which user segment loves the product most, and which segment should you stop serving?

Use with Gustaf's `How to talk to users`, `How to get your first customers`, and PG's "What I've Learned from Users" (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-talk-to-users.md:20`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-get-your-first-customers.md:1`, `../resources/pg-essays/what-i-ve-learned-from-users.md:20`).

## YC Interview Questions

- What do you do?
- Who needs this badly?
- What proof do you have?
- What changed since you applied?
- What did you learn from users?
- Why is now the right time?
- Why are you the right founders?
- Why will this become a big company?
- What is your strongest metric?
- What is the weakest part of the company?
- What will you do if the current plan does not work?

Use with `interview-prep.md` and `indexes/interview.md`.

## Investor Meeting Questions

- What is the company in one sentence?
- What will this round let you prove?
- Why is this fundraise a byproduct of real progress rather than the progress itself?
- What customer or revenue milestone will unlock the next financing?
- What are cash, net burn, runway, and the default-alive/default-dead path?
- If you cannot raise, what changes immediately?
- What is the biggest reason an investor should say no?
- How much dilution are you accepting, and why is that amount worth it?
- Which investor quality matters more than price for this round?

Use with `investor-meeting-prep.md`, Geoff Ralston's seed guide, Michael Seibel's fundraising-rounds note, and Dalton's runway note (`../guides/investor-meeting-prep.md`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/a-guide-to-seed-fundraising.md:68`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/fundraising-rounds-are-not-milestones.md:28`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/advice-for-companies-with-less-than-1-year-of-runway.md:70`).

## Hiring And Team Questions

- Who is the best person you have worked with who might join?
- Have you asked them directly?
- If they said no, who would they most want to hire?
- What will a great engineer find unusually interesting about this company?
- Which part of recruiting can only a founder do?
- If you lack a technical cofounder, what is the plan to create real technical ownership?
- If you are searching for a cofounder, what compatibility evidence do you have beyond profile fit?
- If there is a cofounder conflict, what concrete behavior needs to change, who makes the final call, and what happens if it does not improve?

Use with Harj Taggar's first-engineer and engineer-convincing posts, and Dalton's technical-cofounder note (`../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/how-to-hire-your-first-engineer.md:108`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/how-to-hire-your-first-engineer.md:434`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/convincing-engineers-to-join-your-team.md:1`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/why-you-really-do-need-a-technical-co-founder.md:30`).

## AI Startup Questions

- What customer workflow breaks if your agent is wrong?
- What eval or customer feedback loop tells you the agent is improving?
- What private workflow, data, distribution, process depth, or switching cost compounds as you deploy?
- If OpenAI/Anthropic/Google ships a generic version, what remains specific to your buyer, workflow, or market?
- Are you building AI software, an AI-enabled service, or buying/operating the service business itself?

Use with the AI operating notes, YC's AI Office Hours episode, coding-agent episode, and AI moats episode (`../guides/startup-operating-notes.md`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/yc-partners-answer-your-questions-office-hours.md:33`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-get-the-most-out-of-coding-agents.md:199`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/the-7-most-powerful-moats-for-ai-startup.md:309`).
