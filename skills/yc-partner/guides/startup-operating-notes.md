# Startup Operating Notes

Use this guide for office-hours-style operating advice that is useful to founders but not necessarily part of a YC application review.

## Source Boundary

- Prefer official YC Library and YC Blog sources for operational claims.
- Use PG essays, YC partner posts, Dalton + Michael, and Social Radars as YC-affiliated context.
- Do not turn these notes into admissions criteria. When used during application review, connect them only to a visible application issue.

## Customer Learning And MVP

- Talking to users is a whole-company habit, not a one-time prelaunch step. Gustaf Alstromer says the best founders learn from users throughout the company's life, and user/customer conversations keep founders honest (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-talk-to-users.md:20`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-talk-to-users.md:34`).
- Use user conversations to narrow the MVP. Route user-interview and MVP planning questions through `how-to-talk-to-users.md`, `how-to-plan-an-mvp.md`, and PG's user-learning essay (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-talk-to-users.md:24`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-plan-an-mvp.md:1`, `../resources/pg-essays/what-i-ve-learned-from-users.md:20`).
- First-customer advice should be concrete: who exactly is the first customer, how will the founder reach them, what manual work will they do, and what evidence will prove the customer cares? Use Gustaf's first-customer talk and the Group Partner Lounge customer/payment discussions (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-get-your-first-customers.md:1`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/inside-the-group-partner-lounge-turning-users-into-paid-customers.md:1`).
- For doing-things-that-do-not-scale advice, prefer the official YC Library capture of PG's essay when routing YC Library sources: the core action is manually recruiting users and giving them a good first experience, with at least one founder doing early sales/marketing personally (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/do-things-that-don-t-scale.md:39`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/do-things-that-don-t-scale.md:57`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/do-things-that-don-t-scale.md:326`).
- Christina Cacioppo's Vanta story is a strong office-hours example of discipline before building: her rule was not to build anything else and to keep talking to people until she found someone with a real problem (`../resources/social-radars/christina-cacioppo-founder-ceo-vanta.md:441`, `../resources/social-radars/christina-cacioppo-founder-ceo-vanta.md:454`).
- Use Spenser Skates and Peter Reinhardt as founder-story context for pivots after weak user pull. Spenser says one Solight mistake was not being close enough to users before Amplitude; Peter's Segment story routes through discovering a paying service around customer-data routing and then pushing through awkward customer discovery (`../resources/social-radars/spenser-skates-founder-ceo-amplitude.md:324`, `../resources/social-radars/peter-reinhardt-ceo-co-founder-charm-industrial-ceo-co-founder-segment.md:184`, `../resources/social-radars/peter-reinhardt-ceo-co-founder-charm-industrial-ceo-co-founder-segment.md:288`).
- When a founder dislikes the target customer, probe hard. Christina says liking users makes founders spend more time with them and build better things, which is useful as an office-hours diagnostic for founder-market fit and target-user selection (`../resources/social-radars/christina-cacioppo-founder-ceo-vanta.md:508`).

## Launching And Learning

- If founders are stuck prelaunch, use the YC Library launch discussion as a heads-up note: early launches diagnose problems, find early adopters, and create feedback loops (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/why-startup-founders-should-launch-companies-sooner-than-they-think.md:254`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/why-startup-founders-should-launch-companies-sooner-than-they-think.md:329`).
- A launch that nobody notices is often a learning event, not a disaster. Ask what changed after launch: messaging, target customer, onboarding, activation, pricing, or product scope (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/why-startup-founders-should-launch-companies-sooner-than-they-think.md:254`).

## Focus, Spend, And Default Alive

- Jessica Livingston's "How Not to Fail" is the compact heads-up source for focus, users, growth targets, burn discipline, and denial (`../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/jessica-livingston-s-pretty-complete-list-on-how-not-to-fail.md:110`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/jessica-livingston-s-pretty-complete-list-on-how-not-to-fail.md:188`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/jessica-livingston-s-pretty-complete-list-on-how-not-to-fail.md:230`).
- Use the YC Library "Before growing your startup" article when a founder is trying to pour growth effort into a product that users do not yet love. The note is especially useful for asking whether growth work is amplifying pull or masking weak retention/love (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/before-growing-your-startup.md:30`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/before-growing-your-startup.md:36`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/before-growing-your-startup.md:43`).
- For runway triage, route to Dalton Caldwell's less-than-one-year runway note and the YC Blog version. The first action is to reduce burn or increase revenue before optimistic fundraising assumptions become the plan (`../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/advice-for-companies-with-less-than-1-year-of-runway.md:52`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/advice-for-companies-with-less-than-1-year-of-runway.md:70`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/advice-for-companies-with-less-than-1-year-of-runway.md:1`).
- For spending questions, use the Group Partner Lounge waste-money talk: pre-product-market-fit founders need to earn the right to spend money by first proving a channel or product motion works (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/inside-the-group-partner-lounge-top-ways-startups-waste-money.md:357`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/inside-the-group-partner-lounge-top-ways-startups-waste-money.md:404`).
- Press is a small tool, not a company-building substitute. Michael Seibel's YC Library press note says founders should generate actual news, make their own press contacts, and keep press below a small share of CEO/founder attention (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/getting-press-for-your-startup.md:37`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/getting-press-for-your-startup.md:76`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/getting-press-for-your-startup.md:145`).

## Hiring And Team Building

- Early engineering hiring is founder-led. Harj Taggar recommends exhausting personal networks first, making direct asks, and treating hiring like fundraising by refining the message to candidates (`../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/how-to-hire-your-first-engineer.md:108`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/how-to-hire-your-first-engineer.md:118`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/how-to-hire-your-first-engineer.md:434`).
- Use `convincing-engineers-to-join-your-team.md` when the founder already has candidates and needs to improve offer acceptance, not sourcing volume (`../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/convincing-engineers-to-join-your-team.md:1`).
- For nontechnical founder risk, use Dalton's technical-cofounder note and the existing Dalton + Michael technical-founder resources. Treat it as operating and team-risk guidance, not a blanket application disqualifier (`../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/why-you-really-do-need-a-technical-co-founder.md:22`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/why-you-really-do-need-a-technical-co-founder.md:30`).
- For cofounder search, route factual platform context to YC's cofounder-matching posts and preference data, then pressure-test actual compatibility with relationship and work-trial questions (`../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/y-combinator-launches-co-founder-matching-platform.md:32`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/y-combinator-launches-co-founder-matching-platform.md:70`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/what-do-people-want-in-a-co-founder.md:64`).
- For cofounder disputes, use the Lightcone discussion as office-hours context: conflict is normal, but founders should get concrete about CEO decision ownership, the actual behavior that failed, and whether the relationship can recover under startup pressure (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-navigate-co-founder-disputes.md:117`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-navigate-co-founder-disputes.md:245`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-navigate-co-founder-disputes.md:299`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-navigate-co-founder-disputes.md:520`).

## AI Startup Operating Notes

- Use official YC AI Library pages for AI-specific office-hours questions. Route startup idea questions to `how-to-get-ai-startup-ideas.md`; route AI adoption and product-building questions to `how-to-use-ai-in-your-startup.md` (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-get-ai-startup-ideas.md:1`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-use-ai-in-your-startup.md:1`).
- Keep AI advice tied to customer pain, distribution, data/workflow advantage, and speed of iteration. Do not answer AI questions only with technology trend commentary.
- Use `yc-partners-answer-your-questions-office-hours.md` for AI GTM questions where the founder is choosing between AI software, AI-enabled services, enterprise-first, and midmarket/long-tail entry. It is especially useful for warning against scaling revenue before the manual/AI service loop is understood (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/yc-partners-answer-your-questions-office-hours.md:33`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/yc-partners-answer-your-questions-office-hours.md:69`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/yc-partners-answer-your-questions-office-hours.md:122`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/yc-partners-answer-your-questions-office-hours.md:153`).
- Use `how-to-get-the-most-out-of-coding-agents.md` for AI-building workflow advice: coding agents reward context, verification, decomposition, and organized engineering judgment more than vague prompting (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-get-the-most-out-of-coding-agents.md:199`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-get-the-most-out-of-coding-agents.md:226`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-get-the-most-out-of-coding-agents.md:348`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-get-the-most-out-of-coding-agents.md:539`).
- Use the AI moats episode to push AI founders past "OpenAI could build this" or "we have a model" answers. Probe process depth, private workflow/data, switching cost, evals, distribution, and whether the customer pain becomes more important as AI improves (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/the-7-most-powerful-moats-for-ai-startup.md:39`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/the-7-most-powerful-moats-for-ai-startup.md:177`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/the-7-most-powerful-moats-for-ai-startup.md:309`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/the-7-most-powerful-moats-for-ai-startup.md:568`).
- For incumbent or post-PMF companies adapting to AI, use Spenser Skates' Lightcone discussion: founders may need to retrain the organization, force real AI product experiments, and avoid both grift and conservative inertia (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/what-founders-have-to-unlearn-to-become-great-ceos.md:52`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/what-founders-have-to-unlearn-to-become-great-ceos.md:149`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/what-founders-have-to-unlearn-to-become-great-ceos.md:203`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/what-founders-have-to-unlearn-to-become-great-ceos.md:259`).
