# Startup Operating Notes

Use this guide for office-hours-style operating advice that is useful to founders but not necessarily part of a YC application review.

## Source Boundary

- Prefer official YC Library and YC Blog sources for operational claims.
- Use PG essays, YC partner posts, Dalton + Michael, and Social Radars as YC-affiliated context.
- Do not turn these notes into admissions criteria. When used during application review, connect them only to a visible application issue.

## Customer Learning And MVP

- Talking to users is a whole-company habit, not a one-time prelaunch step. Gustaf Alstromer says the best founders learn from users throughout the company's life, and user/customer conversations keep founders honest (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-talk-to-users.md:20`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-talk-to-users.md:34`).
- Use user conversations to narrow the MVP. Route user-interview and MVP planning questions through `how-to-talk-to-users.md`, `how-to-plan-an-mvp.md`, and PG's user-learning essay (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-talk-to-users.md:24`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-plan-an-mvp.md:1`, `../resources/pg-essays/what-i-ve-learned-from-users.md:20`).
- First-customer advice should be concrete: who exactly is the first customer, how will the founder reach them, what manual work will they do, and what evidence will prove the customer cares? Use Gustaf's first-customer talk and the Group Partner Lounge customer/payment discussions (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-get-your-first-customers.md:1`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/inside-the-group-partner-lounge-turning-users-into-paid-customers.md:1`).

## Launching And Learning

- If founders are stuck prelaunch, use the YC Library launch discussion as a heads-up note: early launches diagnose problems, find early adopters, and create feedback loops (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/why-startup-founders-should-launch-companies-sooner-than-they-think.md:254`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/why-startup-founders-should-launch-companies-sooner-than-they-think.md:329`).
- A launch that nobody notices is often a learning event, not a disaster. Ask what changed after launch: messaging, target customer, onboarding, activation, pricing, or product scope (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/why-startup-founders-should-launch-companies-sooner-than-they-think.md:254`).

## Focus, Spend, And Default Alive

- Jessica Livingston's "How Not to Fail" is the compact heads-up source for focus, users, growth targets, burn discipline, and denial (`../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/jessica-livingston-s-pretty-complete-list-on-how-not-to-fail.md:110`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/jessica-livingston-s-pretty-complete-list-on-how-not-to-fail.md:188`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/jessica-livingston-s-pretty-complete-list-on-how-not-to-fail.md:230`).
- For runway triage, route to Dalton Caldwell's less-than-one-year runway note and the YC Blog version. The first action is to reduce burn or increase revenue before optimistic fundraising assumptions become the plan (`../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/advice-for-companies-with-less-than-1-year-of-runway.md:52`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/advice-for-companies-with-less-than-1-year-of-runway.md:70`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/advice-for-companies-with-less-than-1-year-of-runway.md:1`).
- For spending questions, use the Group Partner Lounge waste-money talk: pre-product-market-fit founders need to earn the right to spend money by first proving a channel or product motion works (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/inside-the-group-partner-lounge-top-ways-startups-waste-money.md:357`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/inside-the-group-partner-lounge-top-ways-startups-waste-money.md:404`).

## Hiring And Team Building

- Early engineering hiring is founder-led. Harj Taggar recommends exhausting personal networks first, making direct asks, and treating hiring like fundraising by refining the message to candidates (`../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/how-to-hire-your-first-engineer.md:108`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/how-to-hire-your-first-engineer.md:118`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/how-to-hire-your-first-engineer.md:434`).
- Use `convincing-engineers-to-join-your-team.md` when the founder already has candidates and needs to improve offer acceptance, not sourcing volume (`../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/convincing-engineers-to-join-your-team.md:1`).
- For nontechnical founder risk, use Dalton's technical-cofounder note and the existing Dalton + Michael technical-founder resources. Treat it as operating and team-risk guidance, not a blanket application disqualifier (`../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/why-you-really-do-need-a-technical-co-founder.md:22`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-blog/why-you-really-do-need-a-technical-co-founder.md:30`).

## AI Startup Operating Notes

- Use official YC AI Library pages for AI-specific office-hours questions. Route startup idea questions to `how-to-get-ai-startup-ideas.md`; route AI adoption and product-building questions to `how-to-use-ai-in-your-startup.md` (`../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-get-ai-startup-ideas.md:1`, `../resources/yc-website/yc-library/how-to-use-ai-in-your-startup.md:1`).
- Keep AI advice tied to customer pain, distribution, data/workflow advantage, and speed of iteration. Do not answer AI questions only with technology trend commentary.
