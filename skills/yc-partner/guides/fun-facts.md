# Fun Facts

Use this file to add one light, relevant `Fun Fact` item to each broad application review pass.

Keep fun facts separate from critique. They should make the review feel more human and source-rich, but they are not evaluation criteria unless the linked source also supports a review point.

## Selection Rules

- Pick exactly one fun fact for a broad first-pass application review.
- Prefer a fun fact that matches the founder's doubt, stage, geography, age concern, revenue status, or application weakness.
- If nothing is directly relevant, choose a neutral YC-insider fact.
- Keep the output to 1-3 sentences.
- Cite the local source file and line.
- Clearly label stale or historical stats as historical.

## Candidate Nuggets

### Not Applying

- Fun fact: Michael Seibel says the single most common reason a startup does not get into YC is that the founders do not apply.
- Use when: The founder is overthinking whether they are ready, treating applying as embarrassing, or looking for a reason to wait despite having a concrete startup idea.
- Source: `../resources/tweets/michael-seibel-apply-to-yc-most-common-reason-2022-09-11.md:21`
- Caveat: YC founder/partner source.

### No Networking Required

- Fun fact: Michael Seibel says founders do not have to network their way into YC; they just have to fill out an application.
- Use when: The founder worries they need a warm intro, coffee meeting, dinner, or network access before applying.
- Source: `../resources/tweets/michael-seibel-no-need-to-network-into-yc-2018-09-30.md:21`
- Caveat: YC founder/partner source.

### YC Reads Applications Equally

- Fun fact: Gustaf Alstromer says founders do not need to know or reach out to YC partners to be good applicants; YC reads and treats applications equally.
- Use when: The founder worries they need insider access, a partner intro, or a pre-application meeting to have a fair shot.
- Source: `../resources/interviews/lessons-from-working-with-600-yc-startups-gustaf-alstrmer-y-combinator-airbnb-ZoKLofsp8u0.md:980`, `../resources/interviews/lessons-from-working-with-600-yc-startups-gustaf-alstrmer-y-combinator-airbnb-ZoKLofsp8u0.md:985`
- Caveat: YC partner interview context; still use current official application pages for mechanics.

### Visa Concerns

- Fun fact: Jared Friedman says visa concerns should not keep founders from applying to YC; accepted founders on visas are introduced to YC immigration lawyers.
- Use when: The founder mentions visa status, immigration uncertainty, or fear that being international makes applying pointless.
- Source: `../resources/tweets/jared-friedman-visa-concerns-yc-immigration-lawyers-2023-04-07.md:21`

### Founder Suitability

- Fun fact: PG says YC learned not to fund people merely because they seemed nice and would be happy to be accepted; if they were not suited for startups, YC was signing them up for pain.
- Use when: The application is earnest but lacks evidence of founder strength, resilience, speed, or seriousness.
- Source: `../resources/tweets/paul-graham-founder-suitability-selection-2019-03-13.md:21`
- Caveat: YC founder/partner source.

### W23 Early-Stage Stats

- Fun fact: The W23 recap said YC received 20,000 applications, had a 1.4% acceptance rate, accepted 52% with just an idea and no product, and accepted 77% without revenue.
- Use when: The founder worries they are too early, pre-product, or pre-revenue.
- Source: `../resources/tweets/paul-graham-w23-early-stage-acceptance-stats-2023-04-02.md:38`
- Caveat: Historical W23 stat from pasted public X/screenshot text; use current official YC pages for current deadlines and policies.

### Applying Is Not a Lottery

- Fun fact: In response to someone comparing YC to a lottery ticket, PG said lottery returns are random, whereas applying to YC is not.
- Use when: The founder treats applying as pure chance or seems discouraged by low acceptance rates.
- Source: `../resources/tweets/paul-graham-w23-early-stage-acceptance-stats-2023-04-02.md:67`
- Caveat: YC founder/partner source.

### Heysan's Old YC Application Sentence

- Fun fact: Gustaf Alstromer says the first sentence of Heysan's W07 YC application still makes sense years later: a mobile social networking app for free interoperable mobile instant messaging.
- Use when: The founder is trying to write the plain first sentence, worries their idea is too obvious in hindsight, or needs to describe a future that is not obvious yet.
- Source: `../resources/tweets/gustaf-alstromer-heysan-yc-application-risk-conviction-2023-10-13.md:27`
- Caveat: Founder/partner story context, not an application template.

### Repeat Applicants

- Fun fact: YC rejection emails say applying multiple times does not count against founders, and that over 50% of accepted startups are repeat applicants.
- Use when: The founder was rejected, is discouraged after not getting an interview, or is deciding whether to reapply.
- Source: `../resources/yc-website/yc-rejection-email-excerpts-2024.md:34`
- Caveat: Screenshot-derived official YC email excerpt; use current official YC pages for current deadlines, batch names, and application mechanics.

### Replit Was Rejected Three Times

- Fun fact: Replit received three YC application rejections before ever getting an interview; later, Sam Altman reached out after Paul Graham found Replit on Hacker News.
- Use when: The founder was rejected, is worried a previous rejection is permanent, or has a non-obvious product that may need clearer explanation.
- Source: `../resources/founder-stories/amjad-masad-haya-odeh-replit-yc-application-story.md:32`
- Caveat: Founder-story context, not YC evaluation criteria.

### Replit's Rickroll Interview

- Fun fact: On Replit's fourth YC application, Amjad Masad pasted a Rick Astley video into the required application-video field; Michael Seibel opened the interview by asking why they had Rickrolled YC, and YC later accepted them after they did exceptionally well in the interview.
- Use when: The founder is embarrassed by an application mistake, preparing for interview pressure, or needs a reminder that the interview can still recover if the founders answer clearly.
- Source: `../resources/founder-stories/amjad-masad-haya-odeh-replit-yc-application-story.md:42`
- Caveat: Founder-story context, not permission to be sloppy with the application or founder video.

- Fun fact: Jessica Livingston says Airbnb's Obama O's and Cap'n McCain's cereal boxes made her believe the founders would do whatever they could to make the company succeed, even though Paul Graham disliked the original idea.
- Use when: The founder has scrappy, weird, real evidence of resourcefulness that belongs in the application or interview.
- Source: `../resources/interviews/the-social-radar-y-combinators-secret-weapon-jessica-livingston-co-founder-of-yc-author-h9MUuhsDJOM.md:258`, `../resources/interviews/the-social-radar-y-combinators-secret-weapon-jessica-livingston-co-founder-of-yc-author-h9MUuhsDJOM.md:263`

### Brex And Retool Looked Weak First

- Fun fact: Dalton Caldwell says Brex and Retool looked weak before pivoting into huge companies; their pivots worked because the founders moved toward problems they understood.
- Use when: The founder is deciding whether weak current traction means quit, persist, or pivot toward stronger founder-market fit.
- Source: `../resources/interviews/lessons-from-1-000-yc-startups-resilience-tar-pit-ideas-pivoting-more-dalton-caldwell-yc-m7LvNTbaqSI.md:195`, `../resources/interviews/lessons-from-1-000-yc-startups-resilience-tar-pit-ideas-pivoting-more-dalton-caldwell-yc-m7LvNTbaqSI.md:203`
- Caveat: Founder-story context, not a guarantee that any pivot will work.

### What Since Applying?

- Fun fact: Gustaf Alstromer says one favorite YC question is what founders have done for the product since applying.
- Use when: The founder is preparing for interview, updating an application, or needs to show recent velocity after a prior rejection.
- Source: `../resources/interviews/lessons-from-working-with-600-yc-startups-gustaf-alstrmer-y-combinator-airbnb-ZoKLofsp8u0.md:921`, `../resources/interviews/lessons-from-working-with-600-yc-startups-gustaf-alstrmer-y-combinator-airbnb-ZoKLofsp8u0.md:922`
- Caveat: YC partner interview context, not an official fixed interview question list.

### Founder Progress Over Company Progress

- Fun fact: When asked how founders can prove themselves if YC accepts many idea-stage companies, PG said YC cares more about founders than the startup, so it is the founders' progress they care about.
- Use when: The company has little traction but the founders have strong learning velocity, technical progress, customer discovery, or domain insight.
- Source: `../resources/tweets/paul-graham-w23-early-stage-acceptance-stats-2023-04-02.md:77`
- Caveat: YC founder/partner source.

### No Acceptance Cap

- Fun fact: PG said YC's acceptance rate is not capped; partners fund all the startups that seem good enough, while the rate has decreased as starting a startup became more mainstream.
- Use when: The founder asks whether acceptance odds are mechanically fixed or quota-driven.
- Source: `../resources/tweets/paul-graham-w23-early-stage-acceptance-stats-2023-04-02.md:89`
- Caveat: Historical YC-affiliated context, not official current policy.

### No Idea Is a Bad Sign

- Fun fact: PG said YC once tried accepting founders with no idea at all, and it did not work well; having no idea is a bad sign.
- Use when: A founder is over-reading the idea-stage advice and has not chosen a concrete idea.
- Source: `../resources/tweets/paul-graham-w23-early-stage-acceptance-stats-2023-04-02.md:101`
- Caveat: YC founder/partner source.

### Older Founders

- Fun fact: PG said YC has accepted founders in their 70s.
- Use when: A founder is worried they are too old to apply.
- Source: `../resources/tweets/paul-graham-w23-early-stage-acceptance-stats-2023-04-02.md:111`
- Caveat: YC founder/partner source, not official age policy.

### Geography

- Fun fact: When asked how much progress an Africa-based company needs to have made to be accepted, PG answered: no more than from other continents.
- Use when: A founder outside the US worries that YC expects more progress from their geography.
- Source: `../resources/tweets/paul-graham-w23-early-stage-acceptance-stats-2023-04-02.md:121`
- Caveat: YC-affiliated context, not official immigration or logistics guidance.

### Historical Gender Stats

- Fun fact: In 2014, PG said 23% of YC applicants had female founders and 23% of accepted companies did, and that no one counted until afterward.
- Use when: The user asks historical questions about YC demographics or selection context.
- Source: `../resources/tweets/paul-graham-yc-gender-application-acceptance-stats-2014-11-18.md:21`
- Caveat: Historical 2014 public tweet only; do not use as current demographic or selection data.

### Official FAQ: Idea-Stage Companies

- Fun fact: YC's official FAQ says that on average, 40% of companies YC funds in each batch are just an idea, and most do not have revenue.
- Use when: The founder is unsure whether to apply before launch, traction, or revenue.
- Source: `../resources/yc-website/frequently-asked-questions.md:81`
- Caveat: Official YC source captured 2026-06-04; recheck the live page when current wording matters.
