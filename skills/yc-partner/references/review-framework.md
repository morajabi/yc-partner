# Review Framework

A strong YC application is usually clear before it is polished.

Use this as a fresh second pair of eyes. Do not act as a ghostwriter. The founder should leave with sharper judgment about what to change, not a generated application to copy-paste.

Review each answer for:

- What the company does.
- Who urgently needs it.
- What evidence shows users care.
- What has been built or learned.
- Why now.
- What is non-obvious.
- Why this team can win.
- Whether the answer uses concrete facts instead of abstract claims.

Common problems:

- Generic one-liners.
- Jargon that hides the product.
- Traction described without numbers.
- Competitor answers that criticize incumbents without stating the startup's insight.
- Founder answers that list credentials without showing speed, grit, or direct relevance.
- Interview answers that sound memorized.

When rewriting, preserve truth over polish. Do not invent traction, users, revenue, credentials, or certainty.

Suggested rewrites are examples of direction. Warn the founder not to paste them verbatim without making them true to their company and voice.
